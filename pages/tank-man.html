Tank Man Timeline

  • April 1989

  • April 15, 1989

    Hu Yaobang's Death

    Hu Yaobang dies, and, because of the delayed government response and funeral, protesters appear in Tiananmen Square.

  • April 20, 1989

    Anger-Elevating Editorial Written

    People become angered by a editorial written in the state newspaper accusing protesters of rejecting the Communist Party.

  • May 1989

  • May 4, 1989

    More people arrive in the square

    Tons of people arrive in the square, mostly students.

  • May 20, 1989

    Troops Move In

    The state army moves in to the square, surrounding them.

  • June 1989

  • June 2, 1989

    Offense Approved

    An offense on the people of the Tiananmen Square is approved late at night.

  • June 3, 1989

    Bloodshed

    The army opens fire on protesters, causing bloodshed, deaths, and many injuries.

  • June 4, 1989

    Anger and Shock

    People in the local area are shocked and angered by the army's reaction to a peaceful protest.

  • June 5, 1989

    Tank Man Intercepts

    Tank man stands in front of a column of tanks, becoming a public figure for the nation and delaying the tanks.

  • June 9, 1989

    Deng Xiaoping Gives Speech

    China's leader Deng Xiaoping appears for the first time, praising military officers and blaming turmoil on counter-revolutionaries.

The Tiananmen Square protest started off with the death of Hu Yaobang, where people gathered in Tiananmen Square to mourn. People got angry with the lack of peace and human rights in China, so the amount of people grew. There were over a million people, so hunger was a big problem. Troops moved in and tension grew. When an offense was approved, there was a night of bloodshed, with hundreds of deaths and thousands of injuries. Fed up with the event, "tank man" stood in front of a tank leading an oncoming convoy. This was seen as an act of extreme bravery, but the man's identity was never confirmed.

The Tiananmen Square protest is connected to human rights in that the protesters were complaining about a lack thereof and that they continued to be denied. The protests entire goal was to bring up topics of human rights such as freedom of speech that were being denied. These protests showed China and other countries that people were angry about their missing rights. Although this protest got the message across, it was not without the loss of hundreds of people, whose deaths defied human rights.
