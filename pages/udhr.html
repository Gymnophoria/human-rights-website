Universal Declaration of Human Rights

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights was created post World War 2. The Universal Declaration of Human Rights was made so no governments can no longer commit any atrocities to their citizens. This pulled citizens into commission. The main disadvantage is that human rights are optional; only countries that agree to do so have to follow them. The other disadvantage is that some human rights were more made for western countries, such as the right to democracy. Edith Roosevelt was one of the main creators of the document, along with the rest of the United Nations in 1948.

  1. Freedom from discrimination. Disallows organizations like the KKK to discriminate against others freely.
  2. Right to equality before the law. Not to be judged by a jury.. wait a second.. Right to a fair trial, so you are not unfairly punished.
  3. Right to remedy by competent tribunal. The right to hold a tribunal, so you can at least try to justify yourself.
  4. Right to a fair public hearing. So you are not illegally arrested.
  5. Right to marry and have a family. So you can settle down and have kids with joy.
  6. Right to own property. So you can have land or a house to yourself.
  7. Right to social security. So you can receive assistance with money when in need.
  8. Right to rest and leisure. So you can go take a nap.
  9. Right to education. So you can learn about the wonderful world.
  10. Right to participate in the cultural life of a community. So you don't feel excluded from a city, town, or other community.
